Town of Danvers Electric Division

Eligibility Requirements & Restrictions:

 $300 maximum in appliance rebates per residential account per year.
 Your electric account must be active, residential, and in good standing. Applications from accounts not in good standing will be held until full payment is received. A DPW representative will contact you for payment.
 Applications require a copy of your sales receipt, Energy Star certification proof, and this form. Incomplete applications will delay processing by 4-6 weeks. Normal processing time for complete applications is 8 -10 weeks.
 Offer is subject to funding and can expire at any time. DED can also terminate the program at any time.

 Appliances must be Energy Star-certified. http://www.energystar.gov/products offers a complete list of certified products. See examples of logo at bottom of page.
 Appliance purchase must be within 1/1/2022 12/31/2022. Applications must be submitted by 1/31/2023 for prior year's program.
 Eligible refrigerators are 15+ cubic feet and must replace an older model. A DPW or retailer recycling/disposal receipt is required. Refrigerators must include both a fridge & freezer component. Stand alone fridges or freezers are not eligible.
 Window AC units must have an EER >12. Proof of disposal required to receive rebate.

 Appliance must be installed at service address location.
 Danvers Electric Division does not warrant, endorse or assume liability for the quality, performance, or safety of the contractor, retailer and/or wholesaler or performance of any appliance or fixture. Responsibility for choosing the product is solely the applicant's. Licensed, insured, qualified installations only. The town of Danvers assumes no liability for installation.

 You agree to allow DED to inspect appliance installation on your premises. Applicant agrees and understands that the Danvers Electric Division does not guarantee that the installation of an Energy Star-certified fixture will result in energy savings, as site-specific energy consumption varies.
